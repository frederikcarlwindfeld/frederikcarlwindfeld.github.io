About

I am a Ph.D. Researcher in the Department of Political and Social Sciences at the European University Institute (EUI), where I study the intersections of politics, security, and technology. My research examines how emerging and disruptive technologies reshape international security, military organisations, global governance, and political authority.

Drawing on critical security studies and international political sociology, I investigate the political implications of artificial intelligence, autonomous weapons systems, military innovation, and software-intensive defence systems. Alongside my doctoral research, I have held visiting appointments at the Swedish Defence University and Stockholm University and have contributed to policy discussions on AI, autonomous weapons, defence procurement, and military technology.

Before joining the EUI, I completed an M.Sc. and B.Sc. in Political Science at the University of Copenhagen. I have previously worked as a research assistant at the University of Copenhagen and the European University Institute, as an intern in development policy at the Delegation of Denmark to the OECD, and as a researcher and project coordinator in the field of intelligence and security.

Publications

My research has been presented at major international conferences, including the International Studies Association (ISA), the European International Studies Association (EISA), the British International Studies Association (BISA), the CEEISA–ISA Joint Conference, the Millennium Annual Symposium, and numerous specialist workshops on international security and military affairs.

In 2025, my work was nominated by the editors of Contemporary Security Policy for the Bernard Brodie Prize for Best Article.

Current Projects

Artificial Intelligence and Military Organisations

Examining how artificial intelligence is being integrated into military organisations and defence systems, and how software-intensive technologies are transforming military capabilities, operational practices, and strategic decision-making.

Autonomous Weapons and International Governance

Investigating contemporary efforts to regulate autonomous weapons systems, focusing on diplomatic negotiations, norm formation, and the challenges of governing emerging technologies amid geopolitical competition.

Software, Defence Procurement, and Military Innovation

Analysing the growing importance of software, systems integration, and supply chains in contemporary military platforms, including research on the F-35 fighter programme and broader transformations in defence acquisition.

Technology, Security, and Political Authority

Exploring how emerging technologies generate new forms of expertise, authority, and governance in international politics and security.
